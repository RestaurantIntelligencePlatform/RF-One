# Desire

**Version:** 0.1
**Status:** Draft

---

# Definition

A **Desire** is the result of an abstract reasoning process that identifies a potentially valuable future business outcome before an executable Process exists.

A Desire may originate from human strategic thinking or from Artificial Intelligence.

Its origin does not affect its nature.

---

# Principles

## 1. Strategic Reasoning

A Desire belongs to the strategic layer of the organization.

It represents a business hypothesis, opportunity or ambition that may or may not become operational.

---

## 2. Independence from Origin

A Desire may be generated by:

- Executive Management
- Business Owners
- Employees
- Customers
- Artificial Intelligence
- External Events
- Regulations
- Any other reasoning source

Its origin is metadata, not part of the Desire identity.

---

## 3. Before Process

A Desire exists independently of any Process.

Only after a Desire is considered feasible may a Process be designed to pursue it.

The corresponding Goal is then created.

---

## 4. Business Knowledge

A Desire is business knowledge.

It represents organizational learning and strategic reasoning.

---

## 5. Persistence

A Desire is never deleted.

Rejected or obsolete Desires remain part of the organization's historical knowledge because future conditions may justify their reconsideration.

---

## 6. Status

Unlike a Process, a Desire has a persistent Status because its evaluation is business knowledge rather than execution state.

Typical statuses include:

- Proposed
- Under Evaluation
- Accepted
- Rejected
- Suspended
- Archived

Organizations may extend these statuses according to their governance model.

---

## 7. Identity

The identity of a Desire is its conceptual meaning.

RF-ONE assigns a unique identifier to represent that concept unambiguously throughout the system.

---

# Relationship with Process and Goal

A Desire does not execute work.

When a feasible operational approach is defined:

Desire
    ↓
Process
    ↓
Goal

The Desire remains part of the organization's strategic knowledge regardless of the future evolution of the Process or Goal.

---

# Summary

A Desire is the bridge between strategic reasoning and operational execution.

It captures business ideas before they become executable capabilities, preserving the organization's decision-making knowledge over time.
