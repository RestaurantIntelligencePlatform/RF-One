# Brand

**Document Version:** 2.1
**Status:** Approved
**Module:** Core Domain

---

# Definition

A **Brand** is the commercial identity through which a Corporate presents itself to the market.

It defines how customers recognize, experience and remember the organization, independently of its legal structure or operational implementation.

A Brand represents the promise made to customers and establishes the standards that every Operational Unit must consistently deliver.

---

# Purpose

The purpose of a Brand is to create a consistent, recognizable and scalable customer experience.

A Brand defines:

- Market Positioning
- Brand Identity
- Customer Experience
- Communication Style
- Product Philosophy
- Service Standards
- Brand Values

Operational Units execute these standards.

---

# Position in the Domain

```
Corporate
    ↓
Brand
    ↓
Operational Unit
    ↓
Operational Area
```

The Brand connects Corporate strategy with operational execution.

---

# Identity

Every Brand has a unique immutable RF-ONE identifier.

Mutable attributes include:

- Brand Name
- Logo
- Visual Identity
- Communication Strategy
- Target Market
- Brand Guidelines

---

# Relationship with Corporate

Every Brand belongs to exactly one Corporate.

The Corporate owns the Brand and defines its strategic direction.

---

# Relationship with Operational Units

A Brand is implemented through one or more Operational Units.

Every Operational Unit belongs to exactly one Brand.

Multiple Operational Units may operate under the same Brand while maintaining a consistent customer experience.

---

# Brand Identity

Typical Brand elements include:

- Brand Name
- Logo
- Color Palette
- Typography
- Tone of Voice
- Story
- Mission
- Vision
- Core Values

---

# Customer Experience

The Brand defines the expected customer experience, including:

- Hospitality Style
- Interior Atmosphere
- Food Presentation
- Service Philosophy
- Music
- Dress Code
- Customer Interaction
- Digital Experience

Operational Units deliver this experience.

---

# Product Standards

The Brand establishes standards such as:

- Approved Recipes
- Naming Conventions
- Portion Standards
- Presentation Standards
- Packaging Standards
- Quality Standards

---

# Service Standards

Examples include:

- Dine-In Experience
- Delivery Standards
- Catering Standards
- Retail Experience
- Loyalty Programs

---

# Marketing

Brand communication may include:

- Advertising
- Social Media
- Promotions
- Public Relations
- Community Engagement
- Partnerships
- Loyalty Programs

Marketing execution may be centralized or delegated.

---

# Intellectual Property

The Brand represents the commercial use of Corporate-owned intellectual property, including:

- Trademarks
- Logos
- Slogans
- Graphic Identity
- Marketing Assets
- Brand Guidelines

---

# Business Rules

- Every Brand belongs to exactly one Corporate.
- Every Operational Unit belongs to exactly one Brand.
- A Brand may be implemented by multiple Operational Units.
- A Brand defines customer experience, not operational execution.
- Brand identity is immutable.
- Operational Units implement Brand standards through Processes.

---

# Future Scalability

The Brand model supports:

- Single-brand organizations
- Multi-brand enterprises
- Franchise networks
- Premium brands
- Value brands
- Virtual brands
- Delivery-only brands
- Future commercial concepts

without requiring changes to the RF-ONE Core Domain.
